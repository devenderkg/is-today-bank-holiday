Bank Holidays In India 2024

In India, banks typically remain closed on public holidays, which can be either specific to a particular state or observed nationwide. All-India holidays comprise significant events such as Republic Day (January 26), Independence Day (August 15), and Gandhi Jayanti (October 2). Additionally, various festivals like Diwali, Christmas, Eid, Guru Nanak Jayanti, Good Friday, and others are recognized as bank holidays. Moreover, the second and fourth Saturdays of each month are designated as holidays for banks.

Bank Holidays In 2024 On The Second And Fourth Saturdays

Bank holidays in India are determined by the Reserve Bank of India. This policy applies to various types of banks, such as private sector banks, public sector banks, cooperative banks, regional banks, and rural banks. All these banks, whether scheduled or non-scheduled, observe holidays on the second and fourth Saturday of each month.
